Majestic Forts of India

Chittorgarh Fort

The largest fort in India, Chittorgarh Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site that exemplifies Rajput architecture and valor. Spread over 700 acres, this magnificent structure stands atop a 180m high hill and features stunning palaces, temples, and towers.

Amber Fort

Located in Jaipur, the Amber Fort is known for its artistic Hindu style elements with its large ramparts, series of gates and cobbled paths. The fort overlooks Maota Lake and is constructed of red sandstone and marble.

Golconda Fort

Golconda Fort is a massive fortress whose ruins stand proudly on a 400-foot-high hill. It was the capital of the medieval sultanate of the Qutb Shahi dynasty and is famous for its acoustic effects and the legendary Koh-i-Noor diamond.

Gwalior Fort

Known as the 'Gibraltar of India', Gwalior Fort is an architectural marvel that has witnessed numerous battles and changes of power. The fort complex contains beautiful palaces, temples, and water tanks, showcasing a blend of Hindu and Islamic architecture.

Legendary Indian Warriors & Kings

Maharana Pratap

The Lion of Mewar (1540-1597)

Known for his fierce resistance against the Mughal Empire and the historic Battle of Haldighati. His legendary horse Chetak became a symbol of Rajput valor.

Chhatrapati Shivaji

Founder of Maratha Empire (1630-1680)

Known for establishing a competent and progressive civil rule with well-structured administrative organizations. He revived ancient Hindu political traditions and court conventions.

Raja Raja Chola I

The Great Chola Emperor (985-1014 CE)

One of the greatest kings of the Chola Empire, known for his military conquests and architectural achievements, including the construction of the Brihadeeswarar Temple.

Rajendra Chola I

The Naval Emperor (1014-1044 CE)

Extended the Chola Empire to its greatest extent, including overseas territories. Known for his naval expeditions and the construction of a new capital city.

Peshwa Bajirao I

The Great Maratha General (1700-1740)

One of the greatest military commanders in Indian history, known for his military tactics and expanding the Maratha Empire to its greatest extent.

Rana Sanga

The Lion of Mewar (1482-1528)

One of the most powerful Rajput rulers, known for his military campaigns and resistance against the Mughal Empire. He united various Rajput clans and established Mewar as a dominant power in northern India.
